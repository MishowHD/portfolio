In March I held **Git 101**, an introductory Git course organized with [POuL — Politecnico Open unix Lab](https://poul.org), the student association at Politecnico di Milano focused on open source culture and Linux.

The course was open to everyone: no prerequisites, just a desire to finally understand what happens when you run `git commit`. I contributed to the slides, worked on the material on GitLab, and held the session in person — also streamed live on YouTube, where over **300 people** tuned in.

It was my first experience with technical public speaking at scale. More than teaching Git, I learned that communicating something clearly to someone who's never seen it before is a skill in its own right — and one I genuinely enjoy.

Politecnico di Milano recognized the activity under the **Passion in Action** program.
